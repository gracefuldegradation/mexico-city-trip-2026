🏠 Check-in — Reynosa 37

Address: Reynosa 37, Hipódromo, Cuauhtémoc, 06100
Neighborhood: Condesa (Hipódromo)
Airport transfer: ~45 mins by Uber (depending on traffic)
Location: Perfect central location — walkable to Roma Norte restaurants, Parque México, and surrounded by cafés, bars, and bakeries. Most dining recommendations are 5–15 min walk.

12:00 PM

Arrive & Late Lunch

Get settled, grab late lunch near the centro. 🍽 Café de Tacuba — iconic 1912 restaurant in historic colonial building with colorful tiles, murals, and live mariachi (Wed-Sun). Famous for enchiladas tacuba, tamales, hot chocolate. Walking distance from Bellas Artes. See dining guide.

1:30 PM

⭐ Optional: Metropolitan Cathedral

Latin America's largest and oldest cathedral — just a 2-minute walk from Café de Tacuba. Built 1573-1813 directly atop the Templo Mayor using Aztec stones. Stunning mix of Gothic, Baroque, and Neoclassical styles. See the gilded Altar of the Kings, 14 side chapels, massive 18th-century organs, and intricate choir stalls. Free entry (small fee for bell tower). Allow 30-45 mins. (Plaza de la Constitución, north side of Zócalo)

2:00 PM

Palacio de Bellas Artes

Art Nouveau exterior, Art Deco interior. Don't miss the Diego Rivera murals on the upper floors. Allow 90 mins. (~20 min Uber from Reynosa 37)

4:00 PM

Choose Your Museum

Option A: National Art Museum (MUNAL) — Mexican art from pre-colonial to 20th century. Short walk from Bellas Artes. Open until 6pm.

Option B: Diego Rivera Mural Museum — Houses Rivera's famous 15-meter mural "Dream of a Sunday Afternoon in Alameda Central" (1947). One spectacular mural depicting 400 years of Mexican history with 150+ figures including Frida Kahlo, Hernán Cortés, Benito Juárez. Quick visit (45-60 mins). Free on Sundays. (~10 min walk from Bellas Artes, adjacent to Alameda Central park)

8:00 PM

Dinner

🍽 Limosneros (Michelin, tasting menu) — see dining guide. Book ahead.

04

Chapultepec Park & Castle

Saturday, April 18  ·  One of the world's great urban parks — ancient forest, a hilltop castle with sweeping city views, and hidden corners to explore all day

9:00 AM

Bosque de Chapultepec — Morning Stroll

Enter the park early while it's cool and quiet. The forest is vast — wander past the lake, botanical gardens and monuments. Bring comfortable shoes. (~15 min Uber from Reynosa 37)

10:30 AM

Chapultepec Castle (Castillo de Chapultepec)

Perched on a hilltop with panoramic views of the city. One of Mexico's most fascinating historical sites — traces the country from pre-Hispanic times through empire and independence. Allow 1.5–2 hrs. Buy tickets online to avoid queues.

1:00 PM

Lunch

🍽 Taquería Orinoco or Polanco restaurants — see dining guide. Short walk from the park.

2:30 PM

Museo Tamayo Arte Contemporáneo

Right at the park's edge — rotating contemporary art exhibitions in a beautifully designed building. A relaxed, unhurried way to end the afternoon.

8:00 PM

Dinner

🍽 Los Danzantes (Coyoacán, Michelin Guide) — see dining guide. Book ahead.

05

San Ángel & Coyoacán

Sunday, April 19  ·  Two adjacent bohemian neighborhoods — historic haciendas, cobblestone streets, colonial plazas (~25 min Uber from Reynosa 37 to San Ángel)

10:00 AM

San Ángel Neighborhood

Start in the charming San Ángel neighborhood. Wander cobblestone streets, browse the Saturday art market (if still running on Sunday), and explore galleries. Beautiful colonial architecture and a peaceful, artistic vibe.

12:00 PM

🍽 Lunch — San Ángel Inn

Reservation: Noon
Legendary restaurant set in a stunning 17th-century hacienda (former Carmelite monastery). Dine in lush gardens with cobblestone pathways and colonial archways. Famous for traditional Mexican cuisine, exceptional margaritas, and romantic atmosphere. Signature dishes: Chile en nogada, tortilla soup, chicken mole. Request patio seating for best atmosphere — it's the best spot. (Diego Rivera 50, San Ángel)

2:30 PM

Travel to Coyoacán

Short Uber ride (~10 mins) or walk (~25 mins) from San Ángel to Coyoacán.

3:00 PM

Jardín Centenario & Jardín Hidalgo

The twin plazas at the heart of Coyoacán — the neighborhood's social and cultural center. Jardín Centenario features the iconic Fuente de los Coyotes (fountain with two bronze coyotes) and is lined with cafes and restaurants. Plaza Hidalgo is the larger cobblestoned plaza with a beautiful Art Nouveau kiosk donated by Porfirio Díaz in 1900. Watch street performers, mariachis, families strolling, browse craft vendors. The plazas are surrounded by colonial architecture including the Parish of San Juan Bautista.

Afternoon

Coyoacán Market & Cobblestone Streets

Browse the covered market for souvenirs, spices and snacks — much cheaper than tourist shops elsewhere. Then wander the neighbourhood's independent bookshops, galleries, and shaded terraces. Grab ice cream at one of the many heladerías or churros from street vendors.

7:00 PM

Dinner

🍽 Cariñito Tacos or El Parnita (Roma Norte) — head back toward your neighborhood for dinner. See dining guide. Festive last night.

Good to Know

Essential Tips

📅
Book in Advance
Frida Kahlo Museum sells out weeks ahead. Private tour guides fill up during peak April season. Do both early.
🚗
Getting Around
Uber is cheap, safe & reliable throughout the city. The Metro is great for longer trips. Avoid the airport currency booths.
🗓️
Museum Closures
Most major museums are closed on Mondays. Plan accordingly — Teotihuacan works perfectly as a Monday day trip.
💳
Money & ATMs
ATMs in the city give the best exchange rates. Pay in pesos wherever possible to avoid dynamic currency conversion fees.
🌞
Teotihuacan Prep
Go early (7–8am), bring strong sunscreen, wear sturdy shoes, and bring snacks. There's no café on-site at the ruins.
📅
Sunday at the Museums
April 19 (Sunday) is your Teotihuacan day — perfect timing, as Mexican residents get free museum admission on Sundays making city museums much busier.
