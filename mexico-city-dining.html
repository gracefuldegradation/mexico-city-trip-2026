Dining Guide · April 15–20, 2026

Where to
Eat

From Michelin-starred tasting menus to legendary taco stands, Mexico City's food scene is extraordinary. Here's where to eat each day.

Essential Info

Dining in Mexico City

📅 Reservations

Limosneros, Fónico, Blanco Colima, and Los Danzantes require advance booking — especially for groups. Book 1–2 weeks ahead for April.

💰 Price Guide

Taco spots: ~100–200 MXN per person. Mid-range: 400–800 MXN. Fine dining tasting menus: 1,200–2,500 MXN. Plus drinks.

🌶️ Spice Levels

Always ask for salsa recommendations. Start mild, work your way up. Most places have multiple salsa options from gentle to volcanic.

Day 1
Wednesday, April 15 — Centro Histórico
Café de Tacuba
Historic
Centro Histórico · Late Lunch
A Mexico City icon since 1912, housed in a stunning colonial-era building. The atmosphere is the main attraction — colorful tiled walls, murals of Aztecs and conquistadors, stained glass ceilings, and old paintings. Live mariachi bands Wed-Sun add to the festive vibe. Perfect first meal to soak in Old Mexico charm.
Signature dishes: Enchiladas tacuba (with spinach sauce & Parmesan), tamales, pineapple empanada (sweet/savory), hot chocolate, traditional antojitos. Stick to Mexican classics over fish/meat.
⏰ 8am–10:30pm daily 💵 $$ 🎵 Live mariachi Wed-Sun
Limosneros
Michelin Tasting Menu Book Ahead
Centro Histórico · Dinner
A Michelin-recognised gem serving elevated traditional Mexican cuisine in a stylish, intimate space. Every dish is thoughtfully prepared with authentic ingredients reimagined for a modern palate.
Standouts: Huitlacoche (corn truffle) tacos, squash blossom with corn smut, chocolate tamale dessert, inventive mole variations
⏰ 1:30pm–10pm 💵 $$$ 📞 Reserve in advance
Day 2
Thursday, April 16 — Templo Mayor & Anthropology Museum
Contramar ⭐
Michelin Bib Book ASAP
Roma Norte · Lunch
Chef Gabriela Cámara's iconic seafood institution, open since 1998. This is one of Mexico City's most celebrated restaurants — buzzy, beautiful, and all about impeccably fresh fish and shellfish. The experience evokes dining at a beachside palapa with an open kitchen, high ceilings, and poetry on the walls.
Signature dishes: Tuna tostada (thin-sliced raw tuna with chipotle mayo, leek, avocado on crispy tortilla — absolutely iconic), pescado a la talla (whole red snapper grilled with red adobo on one side, green parsley sauce on the other), pulpo a la Gallega (octopus with paprika and olive oil), scallop tostada with salsa macha. Strawberry meringue dessert is a must.
⏰ 12pm–8pm (11am Sat-Sun) 💵 $$$ 📞 +52 55 5514 9217 or [email protected]
⚠️ BOOK MONTHS IN ADVANCE

Contramar is one of the hardest reservations in CDMX. Reservations often made 2–3 months ahead, especially for Friday/Saturday lunch. Start calling NOW. If you can get a table for any day April 15–19, take it and adjust the itinerary accordingly.

Taquería Orinoco (Polanco)
Tacos
Polanco · Lunch Backup
A legendary taco spot in Polanco with lines out the door for good reason. Known for their al pastor trompos and signature "costra" style tacos with a crispy cheese crust. Lively, casual, and consistently excellent. Great fallback if Contramar doesn't work out.
Order: Costra-style tacos (cheese crust), trompo (al pastor), campechanos. Crispy fried potatoes on the side. Try multiple salsas — the staff will guide you.
⏰ Opens 1pm 💵 $$ 🚶 Short walk from Chapultepec
Fónico
Tasting Menu Book Ahead
Roma Norte · Dinner
Award-winning cocktails and a superb 8-course tasting menu showcasing elevated Mexican fonda-style cuisine. The space is chic and intimate, and the service is impeccable. A modern take on traditional flavors at a surprisingly accessible price.
Highlights: Queso fundido, pork belly main, tres leches dessert. Cocktails by an award-winning bar manager — ask for a pairing recommendation.
⏰ 1:30pm–10:30pm 💵 $$$ 📞 Reserve ahead
Day 3
Friday, April 17 — Chapultepec Park & Castle
Taquería Orinoco or La Once Mil
Tacos
Polanco · Lunch
Return to Orinoco (see Day 2), or try La Once Mil for a more elevated taquería experience. High-quality sirloin and arrachera tacos on handmade tortillas with excellent salsas. A refined, modern taco spot worth the slightly higher price.
La Once Mil highlights: Wagyu beef taco, arrachera with cheese crust, tres leches cake for dessert. Thick crinkle-cut chips with salsa arrive immediately.
⏰ 12pm–11:30pm 💵 $$ (higher-end tacos) ⚠️ Expect waits 1hr+
Blanco Colima
Book Ahead
Roma Norte · Dinner
A stunning restaurant set in a historic Roma Norte building with soaring ceilings, live DJ, and beautiful lighting. Modern Mexican cuisine with exceptional cocktails. Complimentary artichokes on arrival and dessert to finish.
Standouts: Wagyu (intensely flavored), shaved octopus with Chinese-inspired sauce, salmon with mole. The vibe is as good as the food — upbeat but sophisticated.
⏰ 1pm–midnight (Fri-Sat till 1am) 💵 $$$ 📞 Reserve in advance
Day 4
Saturday, April 18 — Coyoacán
La Valentina
Local Gem
Coyoacán · Lunch
A tiny, unassuming spot near Casa Azul with exceptional home-cooked Mexican food. This is the real deal — authentic, affordable, and beloved by locals. Generous portions, incredible margaritas, and fresh pulque.
Order: Tacos, quesadillas, chilaquiles, guacamole. Try the hibiscus lemonade or a house margarita. Guava mousse for dessert is extraordinary.
⏰ Closed Mondays, 9am–6pm Tue-Wed, till 9pm Thu-Sat 💵 $ 🚶 5 min walk from Frida museum
Corazón de Maguey
Traditional
Coyoacán · Lunch Alternative
Overlooking Jardín Centenario, this restaurant serves excellent mole enchiladas, creative mezcal cocktails, and upscale traditional Mexican fare in a beautiful setting with great people-watching.
Highlights: Three-mole enchiladas, skirt steak tlayuda, guacamole con chapulines (grasshoppers), octopus sausage sope. Extensive mezcal selection.
⏰ 9:30am–11pm (till midnight Thu-Sat) 💵 $$ 🌳 Jardin Centenario location
Los Danzantes
Michelin Guide Book Ahead
Coyoacán · Dinner
Michelin Guide-recommended fine dining right on Jardín Centenario. Beautiful atmosphere with both indoor and outdoor seating, refined plating, and precise cooking. A worthy splurge for the penultimate evening.
Signature dishes: Seafood ceviche, bone marrow sopes, duck, ribeye with smoky wine reduction, apple tart. House-baked croissants at breakfast.
⏰ 12:30pm–10pm (Fri-Sat till midnight) 💵 $$$ 📞 Reserve for dinner
Day 5
Sunday, April 19 — San Ángel & Coyoacán
San Ángel Inn
DiRōNA Gold Book Ahead
San Ángel · Lunch
A Mexico City institution housed in a stunning 17th-century hacienda (former Carmelite monastery). Dine in lush gardens with cobblestone pathways, colonial archways, and elegant blue-and-white Talavera settings. The atmosphere is the main draw — romantic, historic, and utterly beautiful. Saturday brunches are most popular.
Signature dishes: Chile en nogada (considered one of the best in the city), tortilla soup, chicken mole, traditional Mexican cuisine. Famous margaritas.
⏰ 8am–10pm Sun 💵 $$$ 🌳 Reserve patio seating for best atmosphere
Cariñito Tacos
Michelin Bib Asian Fusion
Roma Norte · Dinner
A Michelin Bib Gourmand hole-in-the-wall serving extraordinary Asian-Mexican fusion tacos. Standing room only at a few high tables on the street — it's hectic but absolutely worth it. One of the most creative taco experiences in the city.
Must-order: Pork belly 8 spices, Cantonese-style, Issan, eggplant. Grilled corn ribs (elote). Bright, tangy, bold flavors.
⏰ 12pm–10pm 💵 $$ (~70 pesos/taco) ⚠️ Can take 1–1.5hrs, be patient
El Parnita
Gourmet Tacos
Roma Norte · Dinner Alternative
A beloved Roma Norte institution for gourmet tacos and mezcalitas in a lively, stylish setting. Great vegetarian options and a festive atmosphere — perfect for celebrating the last night of the trip.
Highlights: Hibiscus (jamaica) taco, mushroom tacos, mezcalitas. Extensive cocktail and beer selection. Trendy but unpretentious vibe.
⏰ 1pm–10pm (Sun till 6pm) 💵 $$ 🎉 Festive last-night energy
Taquería Orinoco (Roma Norte)
Tacos
Roma Norte · Dinner Backup
The Roma Norte location of the legendary taco chain (also visited on Day 2 in Polanco). If Cariñito has too long a wait or the group wants something more straightforward, this is a reliable, delicious fallback.
Order: Same menu as Polanco — costra-style tacos, campechanos, fried potatoes. Multiple salsa options.
⏰ Opens 1pm 💵 $$ 🚶 Right in Roma Norte

After Dinner

Bars & Breweries

Mexico City has an exceptional craft beer scene and some of the best mezcalerías in the world. Here are top spots for drinks after dinner.

Lagerbar Hércules ⭐
Craft Lager
Condesa / Hipódromo
A haven for craft lager enthusiasts. This inviting bar specializes exclusively in lagers from Hércules brewery in Querétaro, showcasing a curated selection that rivals the best in the world. Warm atmosphere, friendly service, and a complimentary welcome drink to sample.
Highlights: Hércules Pilsner, seasonal brews, special editions. Food menu includes bratwurst, currywurst, and pretzels. Outdoor seating available.
📍 Campeche 322, Hipódromo ⏰ Opens 12pm 💵 $$
Drunkendog
Craft Beer
Condesa
Part bottle shop, part beer hall — the best beer selection in the city. At least 30 beers on tap with 50+ available in cans. Mostly Mexican microbrews with a few international options. Better-than-average pub food with standout tuna tostadas and ceviche.
Note: Check the daily tap list — kegs run out on small batch beers. Arrive early as it fills up fast, especially on weekends.
📍 Av Nuevo León 4A, Condesa ⏰ Opens 2pm (12pm Sat-Sun) 💵 $$
La Roma Brewing
Brewpub
Roma Norte
Excellent selection of house-brewed beers plus rotating guest taps. The back area with the brewery itself is where it's at — great atmosphere and surprisingly good food. The peanut stout and #6 IPA are standouts. Cauliflower tacos get rave reviews.
Tip: Walk past the front and head to the brewery area in the back for the best seating and vibe.
📍 Av. Yucatán 84, Roma Norte ⏰ Opens 12pm (4pm Mon) 💵 $$
Falling Piano Brewing Co
Brewpub
Roma Norte
Huge brewery with tons of energy, lots of sports on, and a super cool vibe. Wide variety of beers including an excellent light lager. They offer brewery tours and sell merch. Great atmosphere with upstairs dining area.
Brewery tours available: Knowledgeable guides, free pint glass at the end. Book in advance.
📍 Coahuila 99, Roma Norte ⏰ Opens 2pm 💵 $$
Tierra Seca Mezcalería
Mezcal Tasting
Roma Norte
Intimate, moody mezcalería with incredibly knowledgeable bartenders. High-end selection with mezcal tastings and flights. Each pour comes with its own story, origin, and detailed tasting notes. A must-visit for mezcal lovers or anyone wanting an authentic experience.
Tip: Arrive shortly after they open (6pm) — the space is tiny and you'll want a table. The margarita is fragrant and unique.
📍 Av Oaxaca 67, Roma Norte ⏰ 6pm–11:30pm (till 1am Thu-Sat) 💵 $$$
Diablo Negro
Mezcal & Cocktails
Roma Norte
Cute speakeasy-style bar with incredible mezcal cocktails and a vibey atmosphere. The Diablo Habanero and Diablo Verde are standouts — spicy, fresh, and delicious. Speakeasy club upstairs. Limited food (charcuterie board), so plan to eat beforehand.
Hours: Only open Wed-Sat, starting at 4pm (5pm Thu-Sat). Closes late — till 1am Wed, 2am Thu-Sat.
📍 Monterrey 80, Roma Norte ⏰ Wed-Sat only, 4–5pm start 💵 $$
Mezcal y Mole CDMX
Tasting Experience
Roma Norte
Professional mezcal and mole tasting experience in a world-class venue. Certified sommeliers guide you through rare mezcals paired with perfectly prepared moles. This is an educational journey through Mexican culture, not just a bar. Also offers wine, mezcal & ice cream experiences.
Book ahead: Available through Airbnb Experiences or directly. One of the most highly-rated experiences in CDMX. Purchase bottles afterward.
📍 C. Querétaro 33, Roma Norte ⏰ 1pm–8:30pm daily 💵 $$$
